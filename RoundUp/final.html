Weekly Roundup | July 9 - July 13, 2018

Executive Mosaic’s Weekly GovCon Round-Up: $400M+ July Contracts & Top 10 Stories

Jim Garrettson, Executive Mosaic CEO

Five Over $790M

This week, I’d like to highlight major, $790 million+ contract awards. They are:

  • GDMS secures a $3.9B Army contract
  • Booz Allen wins $885M award from the GSA FEDSIM
  • The Navy awards contracts worth $794.5M and $980M.
  • DARPA chooses research teams for up to $1.5B in awards.

      General Dynamics Mission Systems secured a potential five-year, $3.9 billion award from the U.S. Army to provide common hardware systems, GovConWire reported Thursday. The new systems will support military connectivity, interoperability, logistics and maintenance operations. Under the Common Hardware Systems – 5th Generation firm-fixed-price contract, the Army will determine work locations and funds with each order. Chris Marzilli, president of GDMS, is a two-time Wash100 winner.

      Booz Allen Hamilton has won a potential five-year, $885 million from the GSA’s Federal Systems Integration and Management Center, GovConWire reported Tuesday. The company will deliver its eMAPS open systems architecture, which supports enterprise machine learning analytics and persistent services, to customers in the USG Program Office, Department of Defense and intelligence community. Judi Dotson, EVP at Booz Allen, said the company’s data scientists and engineers will provide integrated eMAPS support to warfighters.

      The U.S. Navy awarded 23 companies positions on a potential five-year, $794.5 million contract, GovConWire reported Tuesday. The ID/IQ contract is meant to support the development and fabrication of unmanned underseas vehicles. Contractors will vie for task orders to provide R&D support and help buy services and materials needed to produce UUV systems, the DoD says.

      Additionally, the Navy awarded 22 companies spots on a potential five-year, $980 million contract last week. The firm-fixed-price ID/IQ contract is for the design, build and manufacture of training systems and related devices. Contractors will compete for task orders to modify, test and evaluate training devices for the Orlando, Fla.-based Naval Air Warfare Center Training Systems Division.

      DARPA has selected multiple research teams from industry and academia to receive up to $1.5 billion over five years, GovConWire reported last week. The Electronics Resurgence Initiative seeks to address challenges in microelectronics innovation, through the exploration of new materials and development of circuit tools and systems architectures that increase performance. Companies participating in the program include Northrop Grumman, NVIDIA, Qualcomm and IBM.

 

 

THIS WEEK’S TOP 10 NEWS STORIES

Weekly Contract Awards
This week's top GovCon contract awards.
$10B JEDI Award to Remain Single Sourced
The Department of Defense has announced its $10 billion, Joint Enterprise Defense Infrastructure cloud services contract will stay single sourced, the Washington Business Journal reported Thursday.
Navy Picks 22 Firms for $980M Training System Design, Production IDIQ
The U.S. Navy has awarded 22 companies spots on a potential five-year, $980 million contract to design, build and manufacture training systems and related devices.
Brig. Gen. Lorna Mahlock Named Marine Corps CIO
Brig. Gen. Lorna Mahlock, former deputy director of plans, policies, and operations at the U.S. Marine Corps‘ headquarters, has taken a new role as the service branch’s chief information officer, FCW reported Friday.
Booz Allen Awarded $885M US Government Machine Learning Support Order
Booz Allen Hamilton has received a potential five-year, $885 million contract to help the U.S. Government Program Office an open systems architecture that would support enterprise machine learning analytics and persistent services.
Ellen Lord: DoD Warns Military, Contractors Against Chinese, Russian Software
Ellen Lord, defense undersecretary for acquisition and sustainment, has said the Defense Department started distributing to the military and contractors a list of software platforms that should not be purchased, Defense One reported Friday.
23 Firms Win Spots on $794M Navy Unmanned Undersea Vehicle R&D Support IDIQ
The U.S. Navy has awarded 23 companies positions on a potential five-year, $794.5 million contract to support the development and fabrication of unmanned undersea vehicles.
Air Force Portal Moves to AWS Cloud
An online U.S. Air Force website has migrated from a Defense Information Systems Agency-managed server to Amazon Web Service‘s cloud platform.

Ronald Hahn: AECOM Unveils New Service to Advance Critical Infrastructure Resilience

AECOM has introduced a new service offering that leverages the company’s integrated framework for risk management to increase the resilience of critical infrastructure from cyber attacks.
Leidos Wins Potential $171M DIA Linguist Operations Support Contract
Leidos has won a potential five-year, $170.8 million contract to provide linguist operations support for a Defense Intelligence Agency center that coordinates interagency efforts to examine data from seized foreign paper and electronic documents.

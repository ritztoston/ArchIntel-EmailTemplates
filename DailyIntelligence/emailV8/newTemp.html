Executive Mosaic’s Weekly GovCon Round-Up: Aerospace-Space: Boeing, Northrop and Lockheed & Top 10 Stories

Jim Garrettson, Executive Mosaic CEO

 

Aerospace-Space: Boeing, Northrop and Lockheed

 

As summer has seen a spate of space-related activity, I’d like to recap some of the major platform companies and players consolidating their satellite portfolios.

 

On August 17th, GovConWire reported that Boeing agreed to purchase El Segundo, Calif.-based Millennium Space Systems for an undisclosed sum. The Chicago, Ill., company announced Millienium Space Systems will operate as a subsidiary under the direction of Mark Cherry, vice president and general manager of Boeing’s Phantom Works division. Leanne Caret, president and CEO of Boeing’s defense, space and security business, and a two-time consecutive Wash100 winner, said the addition of Millennium Space Systems will help boost the company’s satellite portfolio and meet the needs of a diverse client pool. Boeing expects the acquisition to conclude by the third quarter of this year.

 

On June 7th, GovConWire reported Northrop Grumman finalized its estimated $9.2 billion purchase of Orbital ATK. Northrop is now operating the former Orbital ATK as its fourth business group, Northrop Grumman Innovation Systems. Blake Larson, former COO at Orbital ATK, serves as corporate VP and president of the division, reporting to Kathy Warden, Northrop’s president and COO, and a three-time Wash100 winner. Warden will assume the title of CEO on Jan. 1st, succeeding Wes Bush, a four-time Wash100 winner. In approving the deal, the Federal Trade Commission mandated Northrop supply solid-rocket motors to competitors on “a non-discriminatory basis.”

 

This week, Lockheed Martin’s GPS III satellite received “available for launch” designation from the U.S. Air Force and is now expected to lift off next year. The GPS III satellite constellation is meant to deliver anti-jamming capabilities to U.S. and allied forces, as well as offer a new L1C civil signal to create global connectivity for commercial users. The company is under contract with the service branch to build 10 GPS III satellites.

 

Earlier this month, the Air Force awarded Lockheed Martin with a $32.1 million contract modification for work on a fourth satellite vehicle in the Advanced Extremely High Frequency program. The AEHF contract – which calls for six geosynchronous satellites to transmit and secure global communications across military assets – has a potential value of $9.2 billion.

Additionally, on August 15th, GovConWire reported Lockheed received a potential $2.93 billion Air Force contract to develop three missile warning satellites. The satellites are expected to succeed the branch’s Space Based Infrared System, as part of the DoD’s Next-Gen OPIR initiative, slated for completion by April 30, 2021. Lockheed’s space systems business will analyze requirements, procure critical path flight hardware and conduct risk reduction efforts ahead of a system critical design review. In May, the Air Force announced its selection of Lockheed and Northrop to the Next-Gen OPIR program.

Weekly Contract Awards
This week's top GovCon contract awards.
CACI Closes $84M Buy of General Dynamics Business Unit; Ken Asbury, Jack London Quoted
CACI International  has closed its purchase of CSRA’s former systems engineering and acquisition services business unit from General Dynamics for $84M to expand support work for the U.S. Navy. 
SAIC to Continue Army Experimentation Support Under $52M Task Order; Jim Scanlon Comments
Science Applications International Corp. has received a potential five-year, $52M task order to help a U.S. Army laboratory facilitate arms experimentation activities via live, virtual and game-based platforms.
Larry Prior: GovCon Industry Could See ‘Another Wave of Consolidation’
Former CSRA CEO Larry Prior, a four-time Wash100 winner, has said the federal government’s spending plans and demand for novel technology platforms will likely drive more mergers and acquisitions among contracting firms, the Washington Business Journal reported Friday.
Lockheed-KAI Team Submits Final Bid for Air Force T-X Trainer Program
Lockheed Martin and Korea Aerospace Industries have submitted a final proposal for the U.S. Air Force’s $16B T-X trainer aircraft contract, The Korea Times reported Thursday.
Report: HHS Appoints Ed Simcox as Acting CIO
Ed Simcox, chief technology officer of the Department of Health and Human Services, has been appointed to serve as HHS chief information officer on an acting basis, Federal News Radio reported Monday.
DoD Appoints IT Vet Michael Conlin as Chief Data Officer
Michael Conlin, vice president and chief technology officer for defense at Perspecta, has been named the first chief data officer of the Defense Department, FedScoop reported Thursday.
Army Taps 5 Firms for Big Data Mgmt Dashboard Contracts
Five companies have won contracts worth up to $1M each from the U.S. Army to build a big data management platform designed to integrate and analyze information from various data sources and carry out forecasting and predictive analytics functions.
WSJ: Trump Reverses Obama-Era Directive on Cyberweapons
President Trump has revoked an Obama-era policy that required government agencies to seek approval from various federal stakeholders before engaging in offensive cyber operations, The Wall Street Journal reported Wednesday.
ManTech to Help Army Build Cyber Training Platform; Kevin Phillips Quoted
ManTech International will collaborate with three other companies to build a prototype for the U.S. Army‘s Persistent Cyber Training Environment under an “other transaction” agreement with Consortium Management Group.
